1. Project Summary

Finding the Right Makers at the Right Budget

The Problem

When our teams need to find creators—photographers, filmmakers, illustrators, animators—for projects that don't require high-end talent budgets, the search process is inefficient. We rely on memory, word-of-mouth, and manual searches across scattered directories. Meanwhile, our best producers and creative directors carry invaluable knowledge about emerging talent that isn't systematically captured anywhere.

The Solution

We're building a searchable database of mid-tier and emerging creators, organized around how our people actually think and search. Rather than rigid categories, the system uses flexible tagging that mirrors creative intuition: "I need a social video like that scene in Sinners" or "someone who shoots like Annie Leibovitz but at a mid-tier budget."

How It Works

Three-Layer Architecture

Hard Fields — The basics every record needs: primary medium (still/video/audio), budget tier, location, contact info, last active date.

Extractable Tags — Attributes scraped automatically from creator profiles, bios, and directories: subject matter (food, automotive, fashion), approach (documentary, narrative, conceptual), visual style (cinematic, gritty, minimalist), tone (aspirational, playful, edgy), and platform fit (broadcast, social, digital).

AI-Derived Attributes — Visual analysis of actual portfolio work to extract color palettes, lighting signatures, composition style, and similarity to known reference points.

The "It's Like..." Problem, Solved

When someone says "Spielberg-like," do they mean childlike wonder? Reaction shots with swelling music? VFX spectacle? We've built a reference library of 100+ industry luminaries—directors, photographers, artists—decomposed into their specific visual and tonal signatures. The system can translate vague references into precise, searchable attributes.
