## Slide 3 — Database role

**What must the database do within the overall project?**

End-to-end flow (left to right):

1. **Database system** (our system)
2. **Client brief** (input)
3. **Creator ID & outreach**
4. **Creator briefing**
5. **Creator execution**

**Within the database system:**

- **Scraper** — *ingest*
- **Analyzer** — *understand / sort*
- **Search engine** — *find / match*

So the database feeds: client brief in → creator ID/outreach → briefing → execution.

---

## Slide 4 — Use cases

**What do users need from this system? How do they want to use it?**

**User:** A creative team or producer or social media strategist needs to find a **creator / maker**.

**Three use-case types:**

**A. Need creators who work in spaces adjacent to a more famous artist**  
- e.g. *"I need someone who paints like Picasso's Blue Period"*

**B. Need creators who work in a particular medium or platform**  
- e.g. *"I need someone who makes documentaries on YouTube"*

**C. Need creators who have a specific topic expertise, POV, or mood**  
- e.g. *"I need someone who can help the client's brand feel more authentic and attractive to Gen Z, particularly when it comes to cars"*

---

## Slide 5 — Database structure (intro)

**What do we need to know about each creator / maker so that we can match them to the need of the moment?**

- **Hard fields.** The basics every record needs: name, primary medium (still/video/audio), budget tier, location, contact info, last active date.
- **"Folksonomic" approach to tagging** — using a dictionary of terms that "ladder up" to tags that describe **the oeuvre of a creator / maker**.

**Classification (primary medium):**

- **STILL:** photography, illustration, ai_imagery, art (painting/sculpture)
- **VIDEO:** film_narrative, commercial, social_platform, documentary, music_video, animation
- **AUDIO:** podcast_spoken_word, music_composition, sound_design
