## Questions for Jonathan & Dan

The following items were identified during a deep analysis of the scoring, categorization, and schema systems. These are areas where engineering made interpretive decisions based on the original project brief — they need stakeholder validation.

### Scoring Algorithm (High Priority)

The engine uses a 15-factor scoring algorithm to match creators to client briefs. All point values below were set by engineering judgment, not stakeholder specification.

| # | Factor | Current Points | Question |
|---|--------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Primary craft match | +30 | Is craft the most important signal? Should it be weighted higher or lower relative to location? |
| 2 | Location match | +20 | How important is proximity? Should remote-friendly creators score differently? |
| 3 | Technical tag match | +10 per tag | Are equipment/software tags (e.g., #ArriAlexa, #Houdini) the right granularity? |
| 4 | Golden Record bonus | +15 flat | Should Golden Records always rank higher? By how much? |
| 5 | Influencer noise penalty | -10 per indicator | Is the penalty too harsh or too lenient? Are the noise keywords correct? |
| 6 | Quality score contribution | qualityScore x 0.2 | What does quality 0-100 actually mean? Who assigns it? What's the threshold? |
| 7 | Style match | +5 per match (max +15) | Should style carry more weight in matching? |
| 8 | Subject matter match | +12 per overlap | Is subject matter (food, automotive, fashion) the right taxonomy? Missing categories? |

### Quality Score Rubric (High Priority)

Currently, `qualityScore` (0-100) defaults to 50 for imported creators with no rubric for what constitutes a 60 vs. 80. The original brief says Creative & Design should do "Visual Vetting" — but no rating criteria exist.

**Questions for Dan/Jonathan:**
1. What makes a creator score 80+ vs. 50? Is it portfolio quality, festival awards, client history, visual consistency?
2. Should the Creative team have a simple rubric (e.g., 5 criteria, each scored 0-20)?
3. Should quality score be auto-derived from portfolio signals, or always human-assigned?

### Categorization Taxonomy (Medium Priority)

The engine categorizes creators across three tiers:

**Tier 1 — Primary Medium:** still | video | audio
**Tier 2 — Subject Matter (19 categories):** food, beverage, automotive, fashion, beauty, lifestyle, portrait, product, sports, travel, tech, medical, architecture, nature, children, seniors, corporate, entertainment, luxury
**Tier 3 — Subcategories (20):** restaurant, luxury-automotive, high-fashion, action-sports, etc.

**Questions for Dan/Jonathan:**
1. Are these the right 19 subject matter categories? Any missing or unnecessary?
2. The subcategory list was derived from an internal deck/docx reference — is it accurate?
3. Should creators be limited to max 10 subject matter tags and max 5 subcategory tags (current limit)?
4. Is the primary medium split (still/video/audio) sufficient, or do we need "interactive" or "experiential"?

### Two Separate Search Pathways (Medium Priority)

The engine has two independent ways to find creators:

1. **Keyword Scoring** (`POST /api/v1/match`) — deterministic 15-factor algorithm, returns score breakdown
2. **Semantic Search** (`POST /api/v1/search/semantic`) — AI embedding similarity, fuzzy meaning-based

These do NOT combine. A brief submitted to `/match` and the same text to `/search/semantic` can return different rankings.

**Questions for Dan/Jonathan:**
1. Should there be a single unified search that combines both? (e.g., weighted hybrid: 60% semantic + 40% keyword)
2. Or is it useful to have both available for different use cases?

### Golden Records Expansion (Blocked)

Currently 11 Golden Records across 8 crafts. The original brief called for 20.

**Questions for Dan/Jonathan:**
1. Can the Creative team identify 9 more benchmark creators?
2. Should there be at least 2-3 Golden Records per craft for better per-craft lookalike models?

### Leo's Demo Feedback (From March 3)

Leo reviewed the live UI and had feedback on:
- Rating values and how scores are displayed

**Specific items to discuss:**

2. Should the engine factor in "recency" — when a creator was last active?
3. Is the "craft over clout" philosophy correctly reflected in the scoring weights?

### Pipeline Flow

```
Cloud Scheduler -> POST /api/v1/scraper/trigger
  -> runPythonScraper() execs scraper/main.py
  -> pipeline.run() iterates SCRAPER_REGISTRY
  -> transformScraperRecords() (src/services/scraper-transform.js)
  -> POST /api/v1/creators/batch -> Firestore
```
